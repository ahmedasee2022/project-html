Cooking tips

Store amazingly in a cool, dark place, as temperature, humidity and light from lightning will cause the seasoning to expire.

Prepare for the next day's meal to save yourself a lot of cooking time, as preparation is the secret to success.

Keep the kitchen, refrigerator and cabinets clean and organized.



HOW TO MAKE IT!

-mango cake wise

1-Easy Sponge Cake: Whisk eggs until foamy, then add sugar, vanilla and whisk at high speed until thick ribbon stage.

2-Fold in flour, corn starch and baking powder.

3-Bake cake in a lined cake pan. Use a sharp knife to cut three cake layers.

4-Whipped Cream Frosting: Whisk heavy cream with powdered sugar and vanilla until stiff peak stage.

Refrigerate until use. Transfer some whipped cream to a piping bag fitted with an open star tip.

5-Mango Cake Filling: Cut mangoes chunks and mix with whipped cream. Scoop some mango balls with a melon baller.

Puree frozen alphonso mangoes or use ready made puree. Heat some puree and add gelatin.

Then stir in white chocolate. Cool and transfer to a squeezable bottle.

6-Assemble the Mango Cake: Spread mango puree over the first cake layer. Dollop half the mango chunk-cream mixture over puree and gently flatten the layer.

Repeat the second cake - puree - mango chunk-cream layers. Place final cake layer on top.

7-Spread whipped cream on top and sides. Smoothen icing using an offset spatula. Place the tip of puree filled bottle at top edge of the cake.

Squeeze and release puree while rotating cake stand. Stop and squeeze to allow the puree to drip randomly.
